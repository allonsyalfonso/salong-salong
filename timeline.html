The chronological tale of Sina Una, from the first session to the final battle.

Bago ang Digmaan: The Age Before
Bago ang Digmaan

Ang Mahabang Kapayapaan

The Long Peace

Under the leadership of Datu Laya, the longest-serving Datu in Sitio Tambis's history, Salong-Salong entered an era of unprecedented tranquility. Tribal conflicts were resolved through dialogue. The sitios prospered. The Salingbobog Council, an alliance of datus and rajas, maintained fragile but functional order across the island. It was a peace built on careful hands and quiet sacrifices.

Bago ang Digmaan

Ang Pagdating ng mga Berbalang

The Coming of the Berbalang

In the shadows of this peace, something moved. The Berbalang, agents of the Berbalang clan of Sitio Pagangpang, began infiltrating the island's power structures, killing and replacing key figures with shapeshifters. Maginoo Adlawan, heir of Datu Adlaw, was among the first to fall. The Tigbanua Buso curse was quietly seeded in Sitio Pasang. The pieces were being arranged long before anyone knew a game was being played.

Ang Simula: The Beginning
Ang Simula

Ang Pista ni Datu Laya

Datu Laya's Festival

On the occasion of Datu Laya's birthday, the sitio gathered for celebration. Among the guests were envoys from Sitio Pagangpang, or so it seemed. At the height of the feast, the aswangs revealed themselves and launched a brutal assault on Sitio Tambis. Sikatuna and Dapithapon fell in the battle. Maginoo Hain was kidnapped. Datu Laya, refusing to accept the loss, used a forbidden spell to resurrect the fallen Maganis, binding their fates irrevocably to the island's crisis.

Ang Simula

Ang Punong Balete

The Balete Tree

The Maganis traveled south into the ancient forest to seek answers at the Punong Balete. The Balatiti, guardian of the great tree, received them cautiously. What they learned in the shadow of those ancient roots pointed them further, toward the sleeping sitio to the west.

Ang Paglalakbay: The Journey
Ang Paglalakbay

Ang Natutulog na Sitio

The Sleeping Sitio

Sitio Anilau lay in collective nightmare. Every resident slept, trapped in a curse of unending sleep, while an aswang wearing Datu Cardo's face tightened its grip on the sitio. Babaylan Dalisay alone resisted the darkness, maintaining her post. The Maganis worked to wake the sitio, navigating the brutal coming-of-age ritual the false Datu had redesigned to kill.

Ang Paglalakbay

Ang Pagdating ni Gabunan

Gabunan's Arrival

Without warning, reality warped. Time stopped. An entity of immense and unknown power appeared, Gabunan, and made its displeasure known. Three Maganis fell: Sikatuna, Mang Tomas, and Ponce. Gabunan toyed with the survivors before departing, leaving behind two scrolls of Revivify as if offering mercy were a game. Babaylan Dalisay, completing a desperate teleportation spell, sacrificed herself to scatter the party to safety. Half landed in Sitio Pasang.

Ang Paglalakbay

Ang Sumpa ng Pasang

The Curse of Pasang

In Sitio Pasang, the Maganis encountered the Berbalang, still wearing the face of Maginoo Adlawan, using a mysterious orb to accelerate the Tigbanua Buso curse among the residents. The Berbalang was defeated in a fierce skirmish. But the curse's roots ran deep, and the suffering of Pasang's people would not end with one battle.

Ang Paglalakbay

Bulkang Betis

Inside the volcano's heart, the Maganis found Ibod trapped in an arcane prism, drained of life by Pian, Sikatuna's wife, who had become something broken and vengeful after her own death. A brutal battle followed. Pian was defeated. In an act of final mercy, she and Sikatuna were laid to rest together, their bodies consumed by the lava as the volcano erupted around them.

Ang Katapusan: The Ending
Ang Katapusan

Ang Ika-39 Salingbobog Council

The 39th Salingbobog Council

The Salingbobog Council gathered at Sitio Malabulak, a summit of datus and rajas meant to forge a new order for the island. What they did not know was that Maria Clara, now fully transformed into a gabunan alongside Isagani and Hain, had arrived not to negotiate but to deliver a reckoning. The hall became a massacre. The 39th Council would be the last of its kind for a generation.

Ang Katapusan

Ang Malabulak Crisis

The massacre at Malabulak sent shockwaves through every sitio on the island. It was not merely an act of violence, it was the destruction of the island's political architecture. Datus who survived fled or fortified. Old alliances collapsed overnight. The event that followed, the Banaba Conflict, was the direct consequence of that night's blood.

Ang Bagong Panahon: The New Era
Ang Bagong Panahon

Ang Banaba Conflict

The Banaba Conflict

From the ashes of the Malabulak Crisis rose something far worse than any single enemy: a sustained, multi-sided conflict between humans, spirits, and aswangs, each faction locked in cycles of retaliation with no clear end. Rea built the new Salingbobog Council from scattered survivors. Ynio worked to break the Tigbanua Buso curse in Pasang. Dapithapon stood watch at Punong Balete. The island did not recover. It endured. And the Banaba Conflict continues, into the era where new stories are yet to be written.

Ang mga pangyayaring ito ang pundasyon ng bagong kabanata. Ang Banaba Conflict ay hindi pa tapos, at kayo ang susunod na magsasalaysay ng kasaysayan ng Salong-Salong.

These events are the foundation of the new chapter. The Banaba Conflict is not yet over, and you are the next to write the history of Salong-Salong.
