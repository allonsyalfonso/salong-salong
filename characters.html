The people who shaped the history of Isla ng Salong-Salong.

Buhay · Alive

BABAYLAN LIHAM

Babaylan ng Sitio Tambis

Alive

Babaylan Liham, in his mid-30s, beams with contagious cheerfulness, relishing in the exchange of rumors and chismis. His deep spirituality intertwines with a profound love for nature and animals, radiating warmth and joy in every interaction. He is often found with his Abayan, a Pilandok. Babaylan Liham was left as the temporary leader and main defender of Sitio Tambis as the magani ventured out to locate and retrieve Maginoo Hain. Sikatuna, Maria Clara, and Rea left their children in his care.

Sitio Tambis

ANG BALATITI

Katiwala ng Punong Balete

Alive

Though its age is unknown, the Balatiti exists to serve and protect the Balete tree, acting as its spokesperson. Harnessing the forest's mystical powers, it safeguards the tree, ensuring that no evil beings come near. The Balatiti remains ever vigilant and cautious of those who seek to approach. The party visited the tree in hopes of finding answers to their questions regarding the aswangs that raided Sitio Tambis.

Punong Balete

IBOD

Kapitan ng Sitio Tambis

Alive

Ibod served as one of the captains of Sitio Tambis, primarily tasked with reconnaissance and guarding the perimeter of the sitio. He led with a kind heart but balanced it with discipline. As Balanoy's best friend, they often kept each other company. During one of their patrols, they were ambushed by aswangs, almost the entire party was wiped out, leaving Balanoy unconscious and resulting in Ibod's kidnapping. The Maganis eventually found Ibod trapped in an arcane prism inside Bulkang Betis. They freed him, but he has yet to awaken.

Bulkang Betis
Nawawala / Hindi Alam · Missing · Unknown

MAGINOO HAIN

Anak ni Laya

MIA

Maginoo Hain, a youthful scion of Sitio Tambis, embodies the enthusiasm and vigor of youth. In his teenage years, he exuded an air of arrogance and recklessness, fueled by an unquenchable thirst for battle and the pursuit of leadership. He is the esteemed son of Datu Laya and stands poised as the rightful heir and future successor to his father's legacy. During the onslaught of Sitio Tambis, he was knocked out and kidnapped by one of the aswangs. Ever since the festival, his whereabouts have been unknown.

Huli sa Sitio Tambis

DATU CARDO

Datu ng Sitio Anilau

Unknown

Datu Cardo succeeded Datu FPJ, adopting a much harsher stance on the politics of the sitio. He decided to cut down trees in the forest to aid in ship production, drastically altering the surrounding landscape. He also made the coming-of-age ritual more deadly, believing that only the strong should endure. It was discovered that Datu Cardo had been murdered and replaced by a shapeshifter, an aswang, who made the coming-of-age ritual especially brutal in an attempt to undermine the maganis.

Sitio Anilau

GABUNAN

???

Unknown

An unknown creature that suddenly warped reality and stopped time. It scolded the aswang posing as Datu Cardo before attacking the party, killing three of the seven maganis: Sikatuna, Mang Tomas, and Ponce. Maria Clara called out to the creature, asking if she was her grandmother, but received no meaningful response. The creature toyed with the survivors, offering them two scrolls of Revivify. After Sikatuna and Mang Tomas were revived, the maganis were teleported away by Babaylan Dalisay before a second battle could begin.

Sitio Anilau
DM Note

Gabunan is the transformed state of Maria Clara's grandmother. Her connection to Maria Clara is not coincidental, it is the result of a generational pact made with a primordial force. New players should not know this until revealed in the new campaign.

Namatay · Fallen

DATU LAYA

Datu ng Sitio Tambis

Deceased

Datu Laya, weathered by years of battle, carries within him a quiet longing for peace amidst the echoes of strife. Despite his hardened exterior, his heart remains open, extending warmth and camaraderie to all within the village. He holds the esteemed title of the longest-serving Datu in Sitio Tambis, a testament to his unwavering dedication and wisdom. Through his steadfast leadership, he thwarted the specter of conflict countless times and guided Salong-Salong into an era of unprecedented tranquility. Upon finding out that his townspeople and the magani did not survive the onslaught of Sitio Tambis, he used a forbidden spell to revive the very people he served. It was through him that Dapithapon and Sikatuna were brought back from the afterlife.

Sitio Tambis

MAGINOO ADLAWAN

Anak ni Adlaw Berbalang

Deceased

Maginoo Adlawan, the only son of Datu Adlaw, was tasked by his father as representative for his tribe. He brought an envoy with him to Sitio Tambis to celebrate the upcoming festival. He is calculative in his actions and always plans out strategies in case things go wrong. It was found out that Maginoo Adlawan was murdered and replaced with a shapeshifter, an aswang. The Berbalang revealed himself in Sitio Pasang, where he used a mysterious orb to accelerate the spread of the Tigbanua Buso curse. He was swiftly defeated in the skirmish with the Maganis.

Sitio Pasang

BABAYLAN DALISAY

Babaylan ng Sitio Anilau

KIA

Babaylan Dalisay had served as the babaylan of Sitio Anilau since the time of Datu FPJ. She was a revered figure who deeply respected the balance between spirits, nature, and religion. She healed Ponce when he was afflicted by the Geas spell, despite him attacking her. She also provided the magani with a way to escape their battle with Gabunan. Unfortunately, Gabunan struck her down just as she was completing her teleportation incantation, causing the spell to go haywire and teleporting half of the party to Sitio Pasang.

Sitio Anilau

PIAN

Espiritung Naghigante

Deceased

Pian was Sikatuna's beloved wife. After her husband was slain in battle, she pleaded with fellow healers to revive him. While guarding the resurrection ritual, she was struck by an arrow and killed, though her body was never found or buried. When the Maganis arrived in Bulkang Betis, they discovered Pian draining Ibod's life energy for her own gain. She was ultimately defeated by Ahbo, Maria Clara, Balanoy, and Rea. They proceeded to lay Pian to rest alongside Sikatuna, their bodies consumed by the lava as the volcano erupted.

Bulkang Betis
