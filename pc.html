MGA MAGANI

◆ ✦ ◆

The heroes who shaped the history of Sina Una. Their stories are finished, but their footprints remain on the land.

Buhay · Survived

SANTEL

Magani · Manlalakbay

Survived

Santel did not walk into Kasaanan because she was brave. She walked in because she could not stop. Where others saw a boundary between life and death, she saw a door, and she pushed through it, dragging Tsinta with her, defying every rule the underworld had ever written. She and Tsinta became the first humanoids to return from Kasaanan alive, and the mark it left on her was not something that washed off. She emerged quieter, sharper, and carrying something the living world could not quite name.

Sitio Tambis

YNIO

Magani · Manggagamot ng Sumpa

Survived

Ynio could have followed his companions into the darkness they embraced. He chose a different path. He returned to Sitio Pasang, the place that had suffered longest under the Tigbanua Buso curse, and gave the rest of his life to breaking it. Years of preparation. Families lost along the way. Resistance from people too broken to believe in salvation. Through Lira's guidance and his own stubborn refusal to abandon what he had started, Ynio pressed on until the first signs of the curse finally began to break. He never stopped finding it a little funny that fate made him face the same enemies his father once had.

Sitio Pasang

DAPITHAPON

Magani · Kleriko ni Mayari

Survived

A Balete Elf of some six centuries, Dapithapon served Mayari, the Moon Goddess, and carried within him an archive of stories most people had forgotten. He chronicled the Maganis' journey in careful kawayang papel, noting not just what happened, but why it mattered. He died once, was brought back by Datu Laya, and spent the rest of the campaign quietly aware that his time was borrowed. He returned to Punong Balete at the end, as a guardian. His farewell letter remains one of the most complete accounts of the Maganis' journey in existence.

Punong Balete

MANG TOMAS

Magani · Siday

Survived

Mang Tomas was the group's siday, a singer and keeper of morale whose presence made the impossible feel slightly less so. He died at Gabunan's hand, was revived, and kept going. Not much is written about what he thought of that. He kept singing. That seemed to be its own kind of answer.

Naging Ibang Bagay · Transformed

MARIA CLARA

Magani · Gabunan

Gabunan

Maria Clara began the campaign as a warrior and a mother, fierce in battle, devoted to her family, the kind of person who ended fights by collecting the head of the enemy's leader. She ended it as something else entirely. Her transformation into a gabunan was not sudden, it was a slow becoming, accelerated by loss, by grief, and by something old in her bloodline that had been waiting to surface. She did not lose herself all at once. She lost herself in pieces, each one preceded by a moment of doubt she could not hold onto long enough.

Namatay · Fallen

TSINTA

Magani · Kapatid

Deceased

Tsinta was the steady hand that never let go, even when everything she held onto was pulling her under. She journeyed into Kasaanan, the realm of the damned, alongside her younger sister Santel, surviving trials that should have unmade them both. Though she often urged caution, she never once abandoned Santel to face the dark alone. She returned from the underworld changed, carrying marks no living person should bear. In the years that followed, she poured her love into letters, especially into one final letter to Santel that said everything she never found the words for while they were still together.

REA LABRADOR

Magani · Punong Tagapangalaga

Deceased

In the wreckage of the Malabulak Crisis, someone had to pick up the pieces. Rea did not want the task, but she was the one left standing who knew how. With Babaylan Liham's counsel, she formed a new Salingbobog Council from the survivors, holding together an alliance that had every reason to collapse. She raised the children of the fallen alongside her own. She wielded kindness and fear in equal measure, knowing when each was needed. Her last will was the most practical document anyone had ever read, and somehow also the most devastating.

BALANOY

Magani · Mentala ng Tambis

Deceased

Balanoy of Sitio Tambis was a practitioner of kawayang papel, a form of paper magic capable of transferring power and knowledge between people. He was Ibod's closest friend and one of the most methodical thinkers in the group. He did not survive the final events of the campaign, but he left behind letters, practical, warm, full of the kind of clarity that only comes from people who know they are running out of time. His papag, made of sampalok wood, went to Ibod. His alchemy supplies went back to his maestro. Everything was accounted for.

Sitio Tambis

AHBO

Magani · Alagad ni Lalahon

Deceased

Ahbo served Lalahon, the volcanic goddess, with complete and uncomplicated faith. She died for it. Her ashes were returned to Sitio Amalciga, but instead of honor, she came home to a people who had turned against Lalahon entirely, blaming the goddess for abandoning her most devoted follower. Ahbo's death did not end her story. It became the reason her sitio abandoned the gods altogether, turning instead toward the ancient giants. She became proof of divine failure. That was not what she intended to be.

Sitio Amalciga

SIKATUNA

Magani · Barbaro

Deceased

Sikatuna was a barbarian who had already crossed the black river once, brought back from the afterlife by Datu Laya at the campaign's beginning. He carried the grief of Pian, his wife, who had died protecting his resurrection ritual. Their reunion inside Bulkang Betis was not what either of them would have chosen. He was laid to rest with her in the lava of the volcano that had held her. That, at least, was something.

Bulkang Betis

PONCE

Magani · Kawal

Deceased

First a soldier, then a friend of Datu Laya, Ponce was a specialist in many kinds of weapons and the kind of reliable presence every group depends on more than they say aloud. He died at Gabunan's hands alongside Sikatuna and Mang Tomas. Unlike the others, he was not revived. He is remembered in Dapithapon's chronicles as someone who always knew where he stood.
