TALASALITAAN

◆ ✦ ◆

Ang mga salita, titulo, at konsepto ng Isla ng Salong-Salong.

A

Aswang

Nilalang

A shape-shifting supernatural creature capable of wearing the faces of humans. Aswangs infiltrated positions of power across Salong-Salong, replacing leaders with impostors to destabilize the island from within. They are not a single creature but a category, the most dangerous are those you do not realize have replaced someone until it is too late.

Abayan

Titulo

A companion animal bonded spiritually to a babaylan. Not merely a pet, but a spiritual partner whose wellbeing is tied to the babaylan's own. Babaylan Liham's abayan is a Pilandok.

Anagolay

Diyosa

The goddess of lost things and recovery. Invoked by those seeking what has been taken, forgotten, or destroyed. Rea calls upon Anagolay in her most private writings.

B

Babaylan

Titulo

A spiritual leader, healer, and intermediary between the human world and the realm of spirits. Babaylans hold significant authority in their sitios, more, often, than the datu in matters of the sacred. They carry knowledge of ritual, medicine, and the invisible world that most people will never directly encounter.

Balete

Puno

The sacred fig tree, believed to be home to spirits and the spirits of ancestors. Cutting a balete without permission is considered deeply dangerous. The Punong Balete of Salong-Salong is the oldest and most powerful of its kind on the island.

Balatiti

Nilalang

A mystical bird that serves as the guardian and spokesperson of the Punong Balete. Its age is unknown. It speaks, evaluates, and does not tolerate those who approach the tree with selfish intent.

Banaba Conflict

Kasaysayan

The ongoing multi-sided war between humans, spirits, and aswangs that began following the Malabulak Crisis. Named after the banaba flower, which blooms in the aftermath of destruction. There is no clear victor. There may never be.

Berbalang

Uri ng Aswang

A specific type of aswang associated with the Berbalang clan of Sitio Pagangpang. Capable of complex infiltration and shapeshifting. The Berbalang who posed as Maginoo Adlawan was instrumental in spreading the Tigbanua Buso curse in Sitio Pasang.

Bulkan

Lugar

Volcano. Salong-Salong has two active volcanoes: Bulkang Betis and Bulkang Kamuning. Both are considered sacred and dangerous in equal measure.

D

Datu

Titulo

The chief or leader of a sitio. A datu's authority is hereditary but can be challenged. The datu handles governance, justice, and represents the sitio in inter-community councils. The longest-serving datu in Salong-Salong's recorded history was Datu Laya of Sitio Tambis.

Diwata

Nilalang

Nature spirits that inhabit specific places, rivers, trees, mountains. Unlike aswangs, diwatas are not inherently malicious, but they are unpredictable and command respect. Disturbing a diwata's home without acknowledgment is considered the height of foolishness.

G

Gabunan

Nilalang

A term referring to a specific category of transformed being, one who has crossed the boundary between human and something older, driven by grief, vengeance, or a power beyond ordinary comprehension. Maria Clara's transformation into a gabunan was the result of a bloodline inheritance long dormant. The full nature of gabunan is not widely understood.

H

Habilin

Salita

A last will, final instruction, or dying wish. In the culture of Salong-Salong, a habilin is considered binding and sacred. To ignore a person's habilin is to invite misfortune.

Hilaga / Timog / Silangan / Kanluran

Direksyon

The cardinal directions in Filipino: North, South, East, West. On the Salong-Salong compass rose, these appear as H, T, S, K.

I

Itim na Ilog

Lugar

The black river, the boundary between the world of the living and the realm of the dead. Kasaanan lies beyond it. Sikatuna crossed it twice. Tsinta and Santel descended past it and returned. Those who do so rarely come back unchanged.

K

Kasaanan

Lugar

The underworld. The realm of the damned, where the river of souls churns with the wails of the lost. Not a place of rest but of unending trial. Tsinta and Santel are the only known living beings to have descended into Kasaanan and returned.

Kawayang Papel

Mahika

A form of magic practiced by Balanoy involving specially prepared bamboo paper. Knowledge, power, and spells can be inscribed onto the paper and transferred to or shared with others. It is both an art and a weapon.

L

Lalahon

Diyosa

The goddess of volcanoes, harvest, and destruction. Ahbo served her devotedly until death. Following Ahbo's sacrifice, the people of Sitio Amalciga turned against Lalahon, cursing her name and abandoning her worship.

Lakan

Titulo

A term of noble rank, sometimes used as a name. The children left in Rea's care after the campaign include one named Lakan.

M

Magani

Titulo

A warrior-hero. In Salong-Salong, the term refers specifically to the adventuring party assembled by Datu Laya to rescue his son and investigate the aswang threat. It has since become a broader term for those who take up difficult, dangerous work for the sake of others.

Malabulak Crisis

Kasaysayan

The massacre of the 39th Salingbobog Council at Sitio Malabulak, carried out by Maria Clara and her companions. It was the event that ended the era of attempted peace and ignited the Banaba Conflict.

Mayari

Diyosa

The goddess of the moon. Patron deity of Dapithapon, who served her as a cleric for centuries. Her clerics are healers, chroniclers, and guardians of the boundary between memory and forgetting.

Maginoo

Titulo

A title of nobility, used for sons and daughters of datus. Maginoo Hain is the son of Datu Laya. Maginoo Adlawan was the son of Datu Adlaw, or appeared to be.

R

Revivify

Mahika

A powerful resurrection spell capable of returning a recently deceased person to life. Gabunan distributed two scrolls of Revivify as a taunt after killing three Maganis, an act that only deepened the mystery of the creature's motives.

S

Salingbobog Council

Organisasyon

The governing alliance of datus and rajas across Salong-Salong. Founded in its modern form by Rea Labrador after the Malabulak Crisis. Its 39th gathering was its last under the old order.

Sitio

Lugar

A settlement or community. The basic unit of society in Salong-Salong. Each sitio has its own datu, babaylan, and customs. Alliances between sitios are political, cultural, and often fragile.

Sulay

Pangalan

One of the children taken into Rea's care after the campaign, daughter of Sikatuna.

T

Tigbanua Buso

Sumpa

A curse that afflicted the residents of Sitio Pasang for generations, causing physical deterioration and suffering. The Berbalang accelerated its spread using a mysterious orb. Ynio spent years attempting to break it through ritual.
