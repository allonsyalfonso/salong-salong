Aklatan ng Mundo

Reference guide to the races, calendar, and classes of Salong-Salong.

Tao Human

The most common people of the island. Found in every sitio and barangay, they build, trade, quarrel, and endure.

Balete Elf Elfong Balete

Long-lived and forest-bound, tied to the great Balete trees. Few remain after the wars. Dapithapon was one of the last.

Diwata Celestial Folk

Blessed children of the old gods, rare and mistrusted in equal measure, carrying divine spark in mortal flesh.

Dwende Hill Folk

Small, secretive, and older than memory. They know paths through the hills that no map has ever charted.

Bagong Araw New Sun · Month I

The first month. Festivals of flame and offering mark the return of Araw's warmth after the cold rains.

Tagsibol Season of Bloom · Month III

Planting season. Babaylans perform the Ritwal ng Lupa to bless the soil and ensure a full harvest.

Panahon ng Ulan Rain Season · Month VI

Spirits are most active when the rains come. Travel becomes dangerous. Strange lights appear in the hills.

Huling Araw Last Sun · Month XII

The final month. The dead are honored, debts settled, and feuds resolved before the year ends.

Magani Warrior

Those who take up arms for the sitio. Not all sought the role, some were chosen by circumstance and could not put the blade down.

Babaylan Spirit-Speaker

Mediators between the living and the spirit realm. They heal, curse, and speak with the dead. Their authority is older than the datus.

Mentala Paper Mage

Practitioners of kawayang papel, magic worked through bamboo paper, capable of transferring memory, power, and will between people.

Manggagamot Healer

Skilled in herb, root, and ritual. Some heal bodies. Some heal curses. Some heal things that medicine has no name for.
